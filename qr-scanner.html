Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 18, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how OMT QR Scanner ("we", "us", or "our") handles information when you use our mobile application OMT QR Scanner (the "App"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our practices.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Camera Access

The App requires access to your device's camera solely for the purpose of scanning QR codes and barcodes. We want to be clear about what happens with your camera:

  • Camera access is used only for real-time scanning of QR codes and barcodes.
  • No images or video are captured, stored, or transmitted to any server.
  • No facial recognition or image analysis is performed.
  • Camera data is processed entirely on your device in real-time and immediately discarded after scanning.

1.2 Scan History

The App stores your scan history (scanned codes and their content) locally on your device:

  • Scan history is stored only on your device using local storage.
  • Scan history is never transmitted to our servers or any third party.
  • You can view, search, and delete your scan history at any time within the App.
  • Uninstalling the App will permanently delete all scan history data.

1.3 Analytics Data (Firebase Analytics)

We use Google Firebase Analytics to collect anonymous usage data to help us improve the App. This data includes:

  • App usage patterns (e.g., which features are used most frequently)
  • Session duration and frequency
  • Device type, operating system version, and app version
  • General geographic region (country level)

This data is anonymous and aggregated. It cannot be used to identify individual users. Firebase Analytics is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

1.4 Crash Reports (Firebase Crashlytics)

We use Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash reports when the App encounters errors. Crash reports may include:

  • Device state at the time of the crash
  • Device model and OS version
  • Stack trace and error logs
  • App version information

Crash reports do not contain any personal information, scan history, or camera data. They are used solely to identify and fix technical issues.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

We want to be explicit about what we do not collect:

  • We do not collect personal information (name, email, phone number, etc.).
  • We do not collect or store images or videos from your camera.
  • We do not collect the content of your scanned QR codes or barcodes.
  • We do not collect location data (GPS coordinates).
  • We do not collect contacts, calendar data, or other personal files.
  • We do not require account creation or login.
  • We do not track users across other apps or websites.

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal data with third parties. The only data shared with third-party services is:

  • Anonymous analytics data with Google Firebase Analytics (as described above).
  • Anonymous crash reports with Firebase Crashlytics (as described above).

4. Advertising

The App currently does not display advertisements. If we decide to introduce advertising in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy accordingly and notify users through an App update.

5. Data Security

We take data security seriously:

  • All data transmitted to Firebase services is encrypted in transit using HTTPS/TLS.
  • Scan history is stored locally on your device and protected by your device's built-in security mechanisms.
  • We do not maintain any external databases containing user data.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Scan History: Stored locally on your device until you delete it. You can clear all scan history from within the App's settings. Uninstalling the App deletes all local data.
  • Analytics Data: Retained by Firebase according to Google's data retention policies (typically 14 months for analytics data).
  • Crash Reports: Retained by Firebase Crashlytics for 90 days.

7. Your Rights

7.1 GDPR (European Economic Area Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to Access: You can access your scan history directly within the App.
  • Right to Deletion: You can delete your scan history at any time within the App.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can disable analytics data collection by opting out of Firebase Analytics in the App settings or your device settings.
  • Right to Data Portability: Since scan history is stored locally on your device, you have full control over this data.

The legal basis for processing analytics data is our legitimate interest in improving the App. You may opt out at any time.

7.2 CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: You can request information about the data we collect, which is detailed in this policy.
  • Right to Delete: You can delete locally stored data through the App or by uninstalling it.
  • Right to Opt-Out: We do not sell personal information. There is no need to opt out of a sale.
  • Non-Discrimination: We do not discriminate against users who exercise their privacy rights.

8. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Since the App does not collect personal information from any user, there is no risk of inadvertent collection of children's data.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
